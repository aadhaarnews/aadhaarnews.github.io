How to enrol your children for Aadhaar

  • Aug 4, 2017

After all the Aadhaar-PAN hype most people know how important Aadhaar is and it wouldn’t be incorrect to say that financial life in the future is going to be difficult without it.

As of now it is not mandatory for kids to possess an Aadhaar, but there’s no harm in enrolling them for the same. It could just make their life easier in the coming years. There would be instances when your child would be required to furnish a proof of identity, and Aadhaar could be of great help.

From attending educational programmes overseas to various government scholarships, Aadhaar may help make such proceedings hassle-free for you. A resident of Noida says he had to get his 2.5 year old child’s Aadhaar made as the school he wanted to admit his child to asked for the ID proof. It could also be helpful in the future if you’re looking to open a bank account in the name of your kids or start investing in mutual funds in their name while they are minor.

Currently, guardian’s Aadhaar is mandatory for opening bank accounts in the name of minors. Child’s ID proof and birth certificate is required. Child’s Aadhaar is not mandatory but acceptable in case the Child’s ID proof/birth certificate is not available, as per SBI customer helpline. However, you would need to confirm this with the branch if opening an account.

For investments in mutual funds normally birth certificate of child is required but some fund houses, such as Tata MF, accept Aadhaar too. If you are looking to enrol your child for Aadhaar here’s your primer:

_If age is less than 5 years _

  1. For children below 5 years, you just need to visit the enrolment centre, fill out the enrolment form and enclose the copies of child’s birth certificate with your own Aadhaar card. Make sure you carry the originals too to the enrolment centre.
  2. If the age of applicant is less than 5 years, no biometrics will be recorded, only photograph will serve the purpose.
  3. In this case, child’s Aadhaar will be linked to the UID of his parents. These children will need to update their biometrics of ten fingers, iris and facial photograph, when they turn 5 and 15.
  4. In this case child’s Aadhaar cannot be made without the parent having an Aadhaar first.

_If age is over 5 years _

  1. For children above 5 years of age, fill out the enrolment form, and enclose the copy of birth certificate and school’s identity card or a declaration on the letter head of the institution.

However if a child is not enrolled in an educational institution, a parent’s Aadhaar or a Certificate of Identity with photo issued by Gazetted Officer/Tehsildar on letterhead can be accepted as identity proof. However, birth certificate of child is mandatory in all cases. For address proof, certificate of Address with photo issued by MP or MLA /Gazetted Officer/Tehsildar on letterhead or by Village Panchayat head or its equivalent authority (for rural areas) is also acceptable. No other address proof will be acceptable.

  1. Their biometrics of ten fingers, iris and facial photograph will be recorded at the time of the enrolment and the procedure will be repeated again when they turn 15.

The rest of the procedure of getting the Aadhaar card is similar to that in the case of adult Aadhaar card.

  1. UIDAI has not classified Aadhaar as as Aadhaar for Adult or Aadhaar for child. Citizens can apply for it and obtain it at different ages. . However, if you have got an Aadhaar card before 15 years of age then you are required to get your photo and biometrics recorded again after turning 15. After that you normally do not need to repeat the process. If at a later stage of your life you face a problem (such as finger prints not matching due to old age or other reason), then you can get it updated.

Quoting its new policy, the UIDAI website says, it is issuing Blue Colour Aadhaar (Namely Bal Aadhaar) for children whose age is between 0 to 5 years. However, the officers at the UIDAI helpline and the Enrolment centre gave varying responses to the question of who would be issued blue colour Aadhaar. says that all the new Aadhaar cards for both kids and adults, that are being issued now will be blue in colour.

The above information was checked by the correspondent from UIDAI helpline and re-verified with officials of a local enrolment centre in sector 10 Noida, Uttar Pradesh.
